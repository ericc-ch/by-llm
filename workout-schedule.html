Your Late-Night Workout Plan

Bodyweight Hypertrophy Schedule — Starting 10:00 PM

Important Notes Before You Start

  • Reference Guide: This schedule details specific beginner exercises. For progressions and further exercise details, always refer to Table 1: Bodyweight Exercise Progressions by Muscle Group in the "Home Bodyweight Hypertrophy Guide."
  • Warm-up (5-10 mins before each workout): Dynamic stretches like arm circles, torso twists, leg swings, bodyweight squats (partial to full), cat-cow stretches.
  • Cool-down (5-10 mins after each workout): Static stretches like chest stretch, shoulder stretch, hamstring stretch, quad stretch, child's pose. Hold each for 20-30 seconds.
  • Tempo Example (from guide): 3-1-X-1 = 3 seconds eccentric (lowering), 1-second pause at bottom/stretch, Explosive concentric (lifting), 1-second pause at top/contraction. Adjust based on exercise. For isometrics, "Reps" will indicate hold time.
  • "Near Failure": As a beginner, this means performing repetitions until you feel you could only do 1-2 more *with good form*. Prioritize correct form over hitting higher rep numbers initially.
  • Progression: Once you can comfortably complete all sets and reps at the *upper end* of the rep range with good form and the prescribed tempo for the listed beginner variation, progress to the next variation listed in Table 1 of the guide, or increase reps/Time Under Tension (TUT).
  • Hydration: Drink water before, during, and after your workouts.
  • Listen to Your Body: Late-night workouts can affect sleep for some. Monitor how you feel. If you experience sharp pain during an exercise, stop or regress. Muscle soreness (DOMS) is normal for beginners.
  • Nutrition Timing: Ensure your last main meal is not too close to 10 PM. A light, easily digestible snack 30-60 minutes before your workout might be suitable if needed. Have your post-workout protein/meal, but avoid overly heavy food right before sleep.

Workout Day 1: Upper Body A

(Example: Monday, 10:00 PM Start)

1. Push-up Variation

Beginner Exercise (Choose one): Wall Push-ups (Easiest. Search "Wall Push-ups tutorial") OR Knee Push-ups (Search "Knee Push-ups tutorial")

Target:Chest, Shoulders, Triceps
Sets:3-4
Reps:8-15 (near failure)
Tempo:3-1-X-1
Rest:60-90 seconds

2. Pike Push-up Variation

Beginner Exercise: Pike Push-up (hands wider, smaller Range of Motion) (Search "Beginner Pike Push-up tutorial" or "Pike Push-up hands wide small ROM")

Target:Shoulders, Triceps
Sets:3-4
Reps:6-12 (near failure)
Tempo:3-1-X-1
Rest:60-90 seconds

3. Bodyweight Renegade Row (from Plank)

Beginner Exercise: Knee Plank Renegade Row (Search "Knee Plank Renegade Row tutorial")

Target:Upper Back, Lats, Core, Biceps
Sets:3-4
Reps:8-12 per side
Tempo:2-1-X-1
Rest:60-90 seconds

4. Diamond Push-up / Close-Grip Push-up Variation

Beginner Exercise: Knee Diamond Push-ups (Search "Knee Diamond Push-ups tutorial")

Target:Triceps, Chest
Sets:3
Reps:AMRAP (good form)
Tempo:2-1-X-1
Rest:60 seconds

5. Isometric Bicep Curl (Self-Resisted, 90° angle)

Beginner Exercise: Self-Resisted at 90° (gentle to moderate resistance). Search "Self-resisted isometric bicep curl". Focus on bicep contraction.

Target:Biceps
Sets:3
Reps:30-45s hold per arm (adjust from 10-15s if needed)
Tempo:N/A (hold)
Rest:45-60 seconds

6. Plank Variation

Beginner Exercise (Choose one): Knee Plank hold (Search "Knee Plank tutorial") OR Forearm Plank hold (Search "Forearm Plank tutorial")

Target:Core
Sets:3
Reps:30-60s hold (adjust from 20-30s if needed)
Tempo:N/A (hold)
Rest:45-60 seconds

Workout Day 2: Lower Body A

(Example: Tuesday, 10:00 PM Start)

1. Bodyweight Squat Variation

Beginner Exercise (Choose one): Partial Squats OR Assisted Squats (holding support). Search tutorials.

Target:Quads, Glutes, Hamstrings
Sets:3-4
Reps:10-20 (near failure)
Tempo:3-1-X-1
Rest:60-90 seconds

2. Lunge Variation

Beginner Exercise (Choose one): Static Lunges (hold position) OR Assisted Lunges (holding support). Search tutorials.

Target:Quads, Glutes, Hamstrings
Sets:3-4
Reps:8-15 per leg
Tempo:3-1-X-1 (or hold)
Rest:60-90 seconds

3. Glute Bridge Variation

Beginner Exercise: Standard Glute Bridge (double leg, small ROM initially, focusing on glute squeeze). Search "Glute Bridge tutorial".

Target:Glutes, Hamstrings
Sets:3-4
Reps:15-25 (double leg)
Tempo:2-1-X-1 (2s pause at top)
Rest:60 seconds

4. Calf Raise Variation

Beginner Exercise: Double-Leg Calf Raises (on floor). Search "Double Leg Calf Raise tutorial".

Target:Calves
Sets:3-4
Reps:15-25
Tempo:2-2-X-1 (2s pause top & bottom)
Rest:45-60 seconds

5. Single-Leg Bodyweight RDL

Beginner Exercise: Partial Single-Leg RDL (focus on balance, limited ROM, use support if needed). Search "Beginner Single Leg RDL tutorial".

Target:Hamstrings, Glutes, Balance
Sets:3
Reps:10-15 per leg
Tempo:3-1-2-1 (slow & controlled)
Rest:60 seconds

6. Lying Leg Raise Variation

Beginner Exercise: Bent-Knee Leg Raises (Reverse Crunch). Search "Bent Knee Leg Raise tutorial".

Target:Lower Abs, Hip Flexors
Sets:3
Reps:12-20
Tempo:2-0-2-0 (controlled)
Rest:45-60 seconds

Workout Day 5: Lower Body B

(Example: Friday, 10:00 PM Start)

1. Pistol Squat Progression / Advanced Squat

Beginner Exercise (Choose one): Assisted Pistol Squats (holding support/to chair) OR Full Bodyweight Squats (focus on depth). Search tutorials.

Target:Quads, Glutes, Balance
Sets:3-4
Reps:5-10/leg (Pistol) or 10-20 (Squat)
Tempo:Controlled (e.g., 3-1-2-1)
Rest:90 seconds

2. Challenging Lunge/Single Leg Variation

Beginner Exercise (If no chair): Deep Bodyweight Squat with Pause (2s at bottom) OR Reverse Lunges. Search tutorials.

Target:Quads, Glutes
Sets:3-4
Reps:8-15/leg (Lunge) or total (Squat)
Tempo:3-2-X-1 (2s pause if applicable)
Rest:60-90 seconds

3. Good Morning (Bodyweight)

Beginner Exercise: Partial Good Morning (limited ROM, focus on flat back & hip hinge). Search "Bodyweight Good Morning tutorial".

Target:Hamstrings, Glutes, Lower Back
Sets:3-4
Reps:12-20 (focus on form)
Tempo:3-1-2-1 (slow & controlled)
Rest:60 seconds

4. Standing Calf Raise

Beginner Exercise: Double-Leg Calf Raises (on floor). If easy, use a step for more ROM. Search tutorials.

Target:Calves
Sets:3-4
Reps:20-30 (AMRAP on last set)
Tempo:1-3-X-1 (3s pause at top)
Rest:45-60 seconds

5. Mountain Climbers

Beginner Exercise: Slow Mountain Climbers (focus on form, core engaged, stable hips). Search "Slow Mountain Climbers tutorial".

Target:Core, Quads, Shoulders
Sets:3
Reps:30-45s
Tempo:Fast but controlled
Rest:60 seconds
