At-Home Bodyweight Hypertrophy

Muscle Building Roadmap - No Equipment

Welcome to Your Transformation!

This guide is for achieving significant muscle growth ("get jacked") using exclusively bodyweight exercises at home. No specialized equipment needed.

Covering foundations, nutrition, workout plans, exercise progressions, and consistency tips.

Foundations of Muscle Growth

Progressive Overload

Muscles need increasing demands:

  • More reps/sets.
  • Shorter rest.
  • Harder variations.
  • Increase Time Under Tension (TUT).
  • Train near failure (some sets).

Caloric Surplus

Eat ~250-500 extra calories daily (vs TDEE).

  • Calculate TDEE (BMR x activity).
  • Prioritize whole foods.
  • Adjust intake by progress.

Protein Power

Aim for 1.6-2.2g/kg body weight daily.

  • Supplement if needed.
  • 20-40g per serving.
  • Distribute throughout day.

Rest & Recovery

Muscles grow during rest. Prioritize:

  • 7-9 hours quality sleep.
  • Scheduled rest days.
  • Active recovery (light activity).

Hydration

Crucial for performance & recovery.

  • ~2.7L-3.7L total fluid daily.
  • Drink around workouts.
  • Pale yellow urine = good.

Consistency is Key

A marathon, not a sprint!

  • Adhere to plan diligently.
  • Track progress.
  • Be patient; celebrate small wins.

Workout Session Structure & Key Terms:

Exercise Order: Start with more demanding compound exercises (multi-joint, e.g., push-up variations, squats) that recruit larger muscle groups. Follow with less complex or isolation-focused movements (targeting smaller muscles).
Sets: The number of times you perform a cycle of repetitions for an exercise. Aim for 3-4 working sets for primary compound movements. Accessory/isolation exercises might be 2-3 sets. These are sets taken close to failure after warm-up sets.
Reps (Repetitions): The number of times you perform the movement within one set. Aim for a rep range where you reach, or are very close to, muscular failure with good form (e.g., 8-15 for many exercises, but this can be higher like 15-25+ for easier variations or lower for very hard ones). The effort and proximity to failure are more important than a specific number.
Tempo (TUT - Time Under Tension): This is critical for bodyweight hypertrophy as it increases the work your muscles do. It refers to the speed of each phase of a repetition. A common notation is (Eccentric - Pause Bottom - Concentric - Pause Top). For example, 3-1-X-1 means:
  • 3 seconds for the eccentric (lowering/lengthening) phase.
  • 1 second pause at the bottom (stretched position).
  • X for an eXplosive or fast, yet controlled, concentric (lifting/shortening) phase.
  • 1 second pause at the top (contracted position).
Slowing down the eccentric phase is particularly effective for muscle damage and growth. Aim for a total TUT of roughly 40-70 seconds per set where feasible.
Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets is generally effective for hypertrophy, allowing partial recovery while maintaining metabolic stress. Shorter rests (30-60s) can be used to increase intensity if form is maintained.
Warm-up (5-10m): Dynamic stretches (arm circles, leg swings, bodyweight squats) to prepare muscles & joints. Cool-down (5-10m): Static stretches (hold 20-30s each) to aid recovery & flexibility.

Example Full Workout Set (for one exercise):

Exercise: Standard Push-ups

  • Warm-up: 2 sets of 5-8 reps with easier variation (e.g., knee push-ups) or partial ROM, focusing on form.
  • Set 1 (Working Set): Aim for 10-15 reps with a 3-1-X-1 tempo. Go to near failure. Rest 90 seconds.
  • Set 2 (Working Set): Aim for 10-15 reps (or as many as possible with good form, likely fewer than set 1) with 3-1-X-1 tempo. Go to near failure. Rest 90 seconds.
  • Set 3 (Working Set): Aim for AMRAP (As Many Reps As Possible) with good form and 3-1-X-1 tempo, pushing to failure. Rest 90 seconds.
  • (Optional) Set 4 (Working Set): If including a 4th set, repeat as Set 3.

Note: Adjust exercise variation to hit target rep range near failure. If you can do 20+ standard push-ups easily, choose a harder variation like decline push-ups.

Your Bodyweight Exercise Arsenal

Master form, then progress (reps, sets, tempo, harder variation). Form over quantity, always. Refer to your full guide for detailed progression tables.

Chest

Ex: Wall Push-ups, Knee Push-ups, Incline Push-ups, Standard Push-ups, Wide Push-ups, Decline Push-ups, Diamond Push-ups, Archer Push-ups, Plyometric Push-ups, Pseudo Planche Push-ups.
Tip: Squeeze chest at top, control descent.

Back

Ex: Prone Y-Raise, Prone T-Raise, Prone W-Raise, Superman, Bird Dog, Bodyweight Renegade Row (Plank), Floor Swimmers, Towel Rows (if sturdy anchor point).
Tip: Squeeze shoulder blades, keep neck neutral.

Shoulders

Ex: Pike Push-ups (feet flat -> feet elevated), Wall-Assisted Handstand Push-up Progressions, Shoulder Taps Plank, Reverse Fly (Standing Bent Over), Lateral Raises (with household items for light resistance if desired), Front Raises (similar).
Tip: Control negative phase on push-ups.

Biceps

Ex: Isometric Bicep Curl (Self-Resisted at various angles: 45°, 90°, 135°), Plank with active Bicep Contraction, Bodyweight "Pelican" Curls (advanced, using floor edge or low stable surface).
Tip: Maximize tension in isometrics, focus on mind-muscle connection.

Triceps

Ex: Diamond Push-ups, Close-Grip Push-ups, Bodyweight Triceps Extensions (Skullcrushers - from knees or toes), Bench/Chair Dips (if sturdy surface available), Overhead Triceps Extensions (self-resisted or with towel).
Tip: Keep elbows tucked on extensions and close-grip movements.

Quadriceps

Ex: Bodyweight Squats (progress to Jump, Pause, or Close Stance Squats), Lunge Variations (Forward, Reverse, Walking, Lateral, Curtsy), Pistol Squat Progressions (assisted, negative, full), Sissy Squats (carefully).
Tip: Squat deep (thighs parallel or lower if form allows), keep chest up.

Hamstrings & Glutes

Ex: Glute Bridge (progress to Single-Leg, Marching, or Feet-Elevated), Single-Leg RDL, Good Mornings (bodyweight), Nordic Hamstring Curl Progressions (very advanced, use assistance), Reverse Hyperextensions (on floor or sturdy edge).
Tip: Squeeze glutes hard at the top of bridges/hip extensions.

Calves

Ex: Calf Raise Variations (Double/Single Leg, on step for greater ROM, toes in/out for different emphasis), Seated Calf Raises (improvised with weight on knees if possible), Explosive Calf Jumps.
Tip: Pause at the top, get a full stretch at the bottom.

Core

Ex: Plank Variations (Forearm, High, Side, with Arm/Leg Lifts, Spiderman), Crunch Variations (Basic, Toe Touches, V-Ups, Jackknife), Leg Raises (Bent-knee, Straight, Dragon Flag progressions), Mountain Climbers (various paces, cross-body), Russian Twists (bodyweight).
Tip: Brace core tightly, avoid letting lower back arch excessively.

Tracking Your Gains: Know You're Progressing!

Workout Log

Crucial! Record: exercise, sets, reps, rest, tempo, RPE. Aim to improve.

Body Measurements

Tape measure (chest, waist, arms etc.) every 3-4 weeks.

Progress Photos

Monthly, consistent lighting/attire. Visual changes motivate!

Performance

More reps, harder variations, better tempo? Direct strength signs.

Body Weight

Weekly, consistent conditions. View with photos/measurements.

Non-Scale Victories

Clothes fit better? Feel stronger? More energy? These matter!

The Long Game: Realistic Expectations

Muscle growth is slow. Beginners: 0.7-1.1 kg (1.5-2.5 lbs) muscle/month in year 1 (with consistent effort).

Factors: Genetics, age, adherence, nutrition, sleep, stress.

"Newbie Gains"? Rapid initial progress from neuromuscular adaptation. Enjoy, but know pace slows.

Listen to your body: Differentiate soreness from pain. Prioritize form. Take extra rest/deloads (reduced intensity/volume) if needed.
